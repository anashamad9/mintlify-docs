This guide walks you through everything you need to go from zero to your first live automation: creating an account, setting up a workspace, connecting an app, and using AI Core to describe and activate a workflow. By the end, you'll have a real automation running in the background without any code or template wrangling.

<Note>
  Atmet is currently in alpha and access is invite-only. If you haven't received an invite, join the waitlist at [atmetai.com/waitlist](https://www.atmetai.com/waitlist). The team will reach out as soon as a spot is available.
</Note>

<Steps>

  <Step title="Create your account">
    Go to [app.atmetai.com/sign-up](https://app.atmetai.com/sign-up) and create your account with your email address.

    If you're on the waitlist and have received an invite link, use that link to bypass the waitlist and land directly in the sign-up flow. Once your account is created, you'll be taken to the workspace setup screen.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a workspace">
    Name your workspace — this is your team's shared environment where automations, skills, and connected apps live together. If you're working solo, name it after yourself or your project. If you're setting up for a team, use a company or team name.

    You can invite teammates from the workspace setup screen or skip this step and invite them later from **Settings → Members**. All members share the same connected apps, workflows, and workspace memory.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your first app">
    Navigate to **Integrations** in the left sidebar. You'll see the available connectors — Gmail, Google Drive, Telegram, GitHub, and more.

    Select **Gmail** or **Google Drive** and click **Connect**. Atmet will redirect you to Google's OAuth consent screen. Grant the requested permissions and you'll be returned to Atmet with the integration active.

    <Tip>
      Start with Gmail or Google Drive — they're the easiest integrations to test with and cover a wide range of common business workflows like document routing, email triage, and notification forwarding.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open AI Core and describe your workflow">
    Click **AI Core** in the left sidebar to open the conversational interface. Type a description of what you want to automate in plain language. You don't need to use any special syntax.

    For example, try:

    > "When I receive a Gmail with 'invoice' in the subject line, save the attachment to a folder called Invoices in Google Drive."

    The AI agent will read your description, propose a workflow plan, and ask any clarifying questions it needs — like which Google Drive folder to use or whether to handle emails with multiple attachments. These questions appear as minimal input panels (a folder picker, a dropdown, or a simple text field) rather than full configuration screens.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and activate the workflow">
    Once the agent has everything it needs, it displays the workflow steps for your review. Read through the plan to make sure it matches your intent.

    For any sensitive or outbound action — like sending a reply email or posting a message — the agent will ask for your explicit confirmation before the workflow goes live. This is a one-time design-time check, not something you'll have to approve on every run.

    When you're satisfied, click **Activate**. The workflow is now live.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Watch it run">
    Navigate to **Workflows** in the left sidebar. You'll see your new automation listed with its current status. Each time the trigger fires — a new invoice email arriving, for example — Atmet logs the run, including every step taken, the inputs and outputs at each step, and the total duration.

    You don't need to stay logged in for workflows to execute. Atmet runs them 24/7 in the background, and you can check the run history any time.
  </Step>

</Steps>
