Atmet is built for teams. A workspace brings everyone together under one shared environment — your automations run, skills are reused, and your agents remember context that the whole team contributes to. Whether you're a solo builder or a growing team, understanding how workspace membership works helps you get the most out of collaboration.

## What Is a Workspace?

A workspace is the shared environment where your team's automations, skills, integrations, and memory live together. Every member of the workspace can view and collaborate on shared workflows and skills, and your agents draw on the same context and preferences that your team has built up over time.

<Note>
  Workspace skills are available to all members of the workspace. Personal skills you create outside of a shared workspace context remain private to your account.
</Note>

## Inviting Members

Workspace owners and admins can invite new members by sending an email invitation directly from workspace settings. Regular members do not have permission to send invitations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open workspace settings">
    Click the gear icon in the sidebar to open workspace settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Members">
    Select the **Members** tab from the settings navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter an email address">
    Type your teammate's email address into the invite field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Invite**. Atmet sends your teammate an email with a link to join the workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Teammate accepts">
    Your teammate clicks the link in the email and accepts the invitation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Member added">
    Once accepted, your teammate appears in the members list and has full access to the workspace.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pending Invitations

After you send an invitation, it appears in the members list with a **Pending** status. It stays pending until your teammate accepts or until the invitation expires. You can resend or cancel a pending invitation from the same members view.

## Removing a Member

If someone leaves your team or no longer needs access, you can remove them at any time.

1. Open workspace settings and go to **Members**.
2. Find the member you want to remove.
3. Click the action menu (⋯) next to their name.
4. Select **Remove from workspace**.
5. Confirm the action.

The member immediately loses access to the workspace. Any personal skills or automations they created under their own account remain intact, but they will no longer be able to view or interact with shared workspace resources.

## Presence

Atmet shows you which teammates are currently active in the workspace. Look for the online indicator next to a member's name — it updates in real time so you know who's around when you need to collaborate or hand off a task.

## Shared Workspace Memory

All members of a workspace share the same memory: uploaded files, stored context, and configured preferences. Your agents use this shared memory to perform better over time.

Because memory is shared, it's worth being intentional about what your team contributes. Encourage teammates to document preferences, decisions, and domain-specific context — the more your workspace memory reflects how your team actually works, the more useful your agents become for everyone.

<Tip>
  If you manage multiple workspaces (for example, separate workspaces for different clients or projects), give each one a clear, descriptive name. You can switch between workspaces using the workspace switcher in the sidebar, and a well-named workspace makes it easy to stay oriented.
</Tip>
